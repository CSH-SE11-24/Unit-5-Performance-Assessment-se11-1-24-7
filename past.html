How Did the Global Warming Look Like in the Past?

Because of the enormous size and heat capacity of the global oceans, it would require a significant amount of additional heat to raise the Earth's annual average surface temperature, even if only by a small amount.

Since the pre-industrial era (1850-1900 in NOAA's records), the global average surface temperature has increased by about 2 degrees Fahrenheit (1 degree Celsius), which may seem small, but it represents a significant increase in accumulated heat.
